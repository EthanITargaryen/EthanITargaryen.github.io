On Reinventing the Wheels on Shoulder of Giants

July 10, 2020 2-minute read
blog
hugo • blog

Hence, I am making my personal portfolio, with almost nothing to showcase. Years ago, in high school, I thought myself a great deal of a writer. Actually, the things I wrote then still scares me. Form is temporary, class is permanent, they say. I am getting narcissistic, but that’s how I view my impressive past.

I’d been an attentive science student in my secondary and higher secondary levels. We always do science on “shoulder of giants”. We acquire the knowledge extracted from this beautiful cosmos by those who came before us, and build our epitomes on top of that. We do not “reinvent the wheel”. If Fahim of July 2020 formulated calculus from scratch, that would be of no benefit, for Newton | Leibneitz did it long before Fahim came to this earth.

After a back and forth within myself, I chose to pursue a degree in CSE, burying half of my passions for “pure” physical sciences. I did not wish to be a developer. I wanted to, and still want to, pursue a theoretical career. Studying bioinformatics is giving me thrills these days, where I can feel the power of systemic logic and algorithms on one side, and ultimately, our amazement at the grand scheme of living things, embedded in ATCGs.

But this persuit is an intertwining of theory and development. Although one may enjoy only one, but the other is definitely needed for full thrill.

Now, amidst this deadly pandemic, on a sleepless night, I’m writing this blog, after finishing a rough skeleton of the platform I’m writing on, my personal portfolio, which I could only make reading a blog of a close acquaintance, Zarif, who also recently made his own, far more excellent than this one now, portfolio. Hugo is a great collection of themes indeed.

As a CSE student, should I know the arts and crafts of web designing? The question is baffling me for long. Perhaps, I should, at least to the minimal level. I am such a noob, or more appropriately so lazy that I cannot even feel to modify the existing theme to format my project page to my own liking.

Somebody, somewhere, has done the design I wish to make. Why should I make one new then? Should I not look for someone who already did it and copy it? Or should I start from scratch? In these cases, we reinvent the wheel on the shoulder of giants, in our own quest to be giants.

God bless my portfolio! God bless you for reading thus far!
